## Part 3 — 90-Day Interview Action Plan (45 min)

### The Goal

A signed offer as Senior Data Engineer or Capacity Planning Engineer at a target company, at or above your target compensation.

### Weeks 1-2 (Days 29-42): Active Applications

**Day 1-3:** Applications
- Finalize resume (one page, quantified bullets, emphasizes APM + capacity planning angle)
- Apply to 10-15 target roles — mix of finance (Citi, Goldman, JPMorgan, MUFG, Deutsche Bank) and tech-adjacent (AWS, data platform companies, SaaS monitoring companies)
- LinkedIn "Open to Work" (visible to recruiters only)
- Reach out to 5 former colleagues for referrals

**Day 4-7:** Recruiter screens
- Treat every recruiter call as practice
- Have the "Why this role" story ready for each firm
- Ask: "What does the technical process look like? How many rounds? What topics are covered?"
- Get the job description in writing — map it to your study plan

**Day 8-14:** First technical screens
- Keep practicing 3 LeetCode problems per day (Medium focus)
- Run one SQL challenge per day (write it, run it in DuckDB)
- Review the 2 days of study material most relevant to each firm before that firm's interview

---

### Weeks 3-6 (Days 43-70): Active Interview Loop

**Daily maintenance:**
- 2 LeetCode problems (timed, from scratch, no hints)
- 1 SQL problem
- Review 1 behavioral story out loud (cycle through all 5)

**For each interview:**
- Day before: review the firm's tech stack and company research template (Day 19)
- Night before: review the 3 cheat-sheet lines from Day 20
- Day of: no cramming. Light review of your 5 stories. Eat. Sleep.
- Day after: write down every question you were asked. Note where you stumbled.

**Feedback loop:**
- After every interview, update this document with what was asked
- Any new question that stumped you → study it → add to SR rotation
- Track your pipeline: Applied → Screen → Technical → Final Round → Offer

---

### Weeks 7-12 (Days 71-90): Close the Loop

**If you have offers:**
- Use the Day 26 negotiation framework
- Don't give a verbal acceptance without reviewing the written offer
- Negotiate total comp: base, bonus structure, signing, PTO, start date
- If multiple offers: use them as leverage with each firm

**If you have no offers after 6 weeks:**
- Diagnose: where in the process are you losing? Screen? Technical? Final?
- Screen losses → story or communication issue (return to Day 26 behavioral)
- Technical losses → specific pattern weakness (return to the specific day)
- Final losses → likely culture fit or leadership signals (behavioral depth)
- Consider adding mock interviews with a human (peer practice, Pramp, interviewing.io)

## Part 4 — Maintenance Mode (After Interviews Begin)

Once you're in an active interview loop, stop adding new topics. Switch to maintenance:

### Daily Minimum (20 minutes)
- 1 LeetCode problem (medium, pattern rotation)
- OR 1 SQL problem
- OR speak 1 behavioral story out loud

### Weekly
- Review your target company research templates
- Update the tracker
- Run one timed system design (draw a design in 10 minutes, then critique it)

### If You Get Stuck on a Pattern
- Go back to that day's study plan
- Do the exact problems in that section from scratch, no notes
- If still stuck after two passes: use Gemini to run a micro-mock on that pattern only
