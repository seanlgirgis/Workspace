# Day 90 — First Day Field Guide

**Theme:** Today is not a test. It's an orientation. You're here to absorb, connect, and listen. Everything else comes later.

---

## No Study Content Today

This isn't a study plan day. It's a field guide for navigating the day.

---

## Morning Protocol

**Before you leave:**
- Eat. Don't skip breakfast. Orientation days are long and cognitive.
- Leave earlier than you think you need to.
- Bring: ID documents, notebook, pen, water, small snack. Phone charged.

**In transit:**
- No podcast, no news. Quiet mind.
- Review your 3-sentence intention from last night.
- Remember: you're not going to prove yourself today. You're going to meet people.

---

## The First Hour

**Getting in the door:**
- If you're lost — ask for help immediately. Don't wander for 10 minutes. Someone will be happy to help.
- Introduce yourself to whoever is at the front desk, IT, or reception: first name, team, start date. They're people too.

**When you meet your manager:**
- Say something genuine: "I'm really glad to be here. What should I try to get oriented on first?"
- Follow their lead on the day's structure.

**When you meet teammates:**
- Learn their name + one thing about their work. That's the goal — not more.
- "What are you working on right now?" is the best first question you can ask.

---

## Throughout the Day

**Take notes — on paper:**
Names. System names. Acronyms. Process names. Tools. Things to look up later. Do not rely on memory for any of this today.

**Ask questions — but budget them:**
You don't have to ask every question the moment it arises. Collect them. Ask a few good ones in each conversation rather than peppering everyone.

**Good first-day questions:**
- "What does a successful first 30 days look like from your perspective?"
- "What's the biggest pain point on the team right now?"
- "Is there documentation I should read to get up to speed on the data architecture?"
- "Who are the key people outside this team that I should get to know?"

**Calibrate your talking:**
Senior engineers are sometimes nervous and talk too much about themselves. Listen more than you talk. The goal is to understand, not to impress.

---

## If It's Overwhelming

It will be. Information density on Day 1 is usually absurd.

**When you feel information overload:**
Stop trying to retain. Just note the topic and move on. You'll have access to these people again tomorrow. Today is exposure, not mastery.

**When you don't know something:**
"I don't know that yet" is a complete answer. "I'll look into that this week" is a great follow-up.

**When you make a mistake:**
You will. Everyone does on Day 1 — a wrong door, a forgotten name, a mispronounced acronym. Shake it off. Move on. Nobody's tracking it.

---

## End of Day Protocol

**Last 30 minutes:**

Write in your notebook:
- 3 names I learned today + something about each
- 1 thing I want to understand better tomorrow
- 1 question I want to ask my manager tomorrow
- 1 thing about today that felt right

Don't write a performance review of yourself. Write observations.

**Before you leave:**
Say goodbye to at least 2 people. "See you tomorrow" is enough. It signals you'll be back and you're engaged.

**Commute home:**
No podcasts, no news. Let the day settle. Decompress in quiet.

---

## Tonight

Eat. Rest. If you want to do anything productive: write a few more notes from the day while they're fresh. Set tomorrow's alarm.

You did Day 1.

---

## Day 90 Is Not a Checklist Day

The one measure: Did you learn at least 3 people's names and something about what they work on?

If yes: Day 90 was a success.

Everything else is ahead.
