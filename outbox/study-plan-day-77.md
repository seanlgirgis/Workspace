## Transition Mindset Session (40 min)

### The Mental Shift

You spent 11 weeks in acquisition mode: applying, prepping, performing, negotiating. That mode requires vigilance, hustle, and a slightly defensive posture.

The new mode is different. You're now a future colleague. You've been selected. The job now is to show up ready to add value — not to continue proving yourself.

**Things to let go of:**
- The anxiety about whether you'll get the offer (you got it)
- The imposter feeling from interview prep (the preparation was real, the competence is real)
- The comparison mindset (you made the best decision you could with the information you had)

**Things to carry forward:**
- The structured thinking you built across 11 weeks
- The technical vocabulary and depth you developed
- The habits: daily maintenance, reflection, deliberate practice

### The First 90 Days — Setting Up to Win

Before you start, get clear on your intent:

**First 30 days — Listen and learn:**
- Learn the systems before touching them
- Build relationships before proposing changes
- Understand who the informal power centers are (not just the org chart)
- Ask: "What's the biggest unsolved problem on this team?"

**Days 31-60 — First contribution:**
- Identify one concrete thing you can improve or ship
- Make it visible, but don't overclaim credit
- Show your communication style: clear, written, well-scoped

**Days 61-90 — Establish your operating rhythm:**
- Have 1:1s with key stakeholders outside your immediate team
- Propose one project or initiative (even small) that's yours
- By Day 90: your manager should know what to expect from you
