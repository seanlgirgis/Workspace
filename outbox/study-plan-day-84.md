## Pre-Start Checklist (30 min)

### Logistics

- [ ] **First day location confirmed** — office address, floor, who to ask for
- [ ] **Parking / commute route** — test it if you haven't. Don't be late on Day 1.
- [ ] **Equipment** — if they're shipping a laptop, confirm it has arrived
- [ ] **IT setup appointment** — is there a Day 1 onboarding session? Time? Zoom link?
- [ ] **Dress code confirmed** — ask if unclear. It's not a trivial question.
- [ ] **Lunch plan** — bring something or know where to go. Don't skip lunch on Day 1.

### First Week Agenda (if known)

Write down what you know about your first week schedule:
```
Monday:    ___
Tuesday:   ___
Wednesday: ___
Thursday:  ___
Friday:    ___
```

If you don't know — that's fine. Be ready for ambiguity. Go with the flow but watch for opportunities to introduce yourself to people.

### The Day 1 Mindset

**You are not there to prove you were the right hire.** That decision was already made. You're there to learn.

**You are not there to fix things.** Not yet. Listen first.

**You are allowed to say "I don't know yet."** In a new role, this is not a weakness. A confident senior engineer who says "I don't know that system yet, but I'll have it by end of week" is far more credible than one who fakes it.

**The single best thing you can do on Day 1:** Learn 3 people's names, what they work on, and something about what matters to them. That's it. Everything else follows from relationships.

---

## What to Expect From Yourself in Week 1

**Information overload is normal.** You will not remember everything you're told. Take notes. Ask for documentation. Don't panic when you can't hold it all in working memory.

**You will feel behind.** You are behind — everyone new is behind. The question isn't "am I behind?" It's "am I learning?" Give yourself 90 days before making any judgment about your performance.

**You will have imposter moments.** The 10+ years of APM expertise, the 84 days of study, the interview process that worked — none of it eliminates the first-week feeling of "do I belong here?" That feeling is information, not truth. It passes.
