# Day 89 — The Night Before

**Theme:** You've done 88 days of preparation. Tonight is not about learning anything new. It's about showing up tomorrow as the best version of what you've built.

---

## No Daily Maintenance Today

Skip LC. Skip SQL. If you feel compelled to review something: re-read your behavioral stories once, slowly. That's it.

---

## Night-Before Protocol (60 min total, spread across the evening)

### 6pm — Logistics Lock-In (10 min)

Confirm everything you need for tomorrow:

```
[ ] Office address + floor confirmed
[ ] Who to ask for at reception (HR? Manager? Buddy?)
[ ] Badge/access situation (will someone meet you? Do you have a visitor badge process?)
[ ] Commute route + departure time — leave 15 minutes earlier than calculated
[ ] Parking / transit plan
[ ] Dress code confirmed
[ ] Laptop — will they provide it on Day 1? Or do you bring your own?
[ ] ID documents if required for I-9 / security badge
[ ] Lunch plan
[ ] Water bottle + small snack (long orientation days drain energy)
```

---

### 7pm — Tomorrow's Intentions (15 min)

Write 3 sentences:

**What I want to accomplish tomorrow:**
Not a task list. An intention. Something like:
> "I want to learn 3 people's names and something about what they work on. I want to ask at least 2 genuine questions. I want to end the day feeling like I listened more than I talked."

**How I want to show up:**
> "Curious, calm, present. Not performing — actually there."

**What I'll do if I feel overwhelmed:**
> "I'll breathe, take a note, and remind myself that the overwhelming feeling is day-1 normal, not a signal that I'm wrong for the role."

---

### 8pm — Prepare Your Physical Space (10 min)

Lay out tomorrow:
- Clothes chosen (not left for the morning)
- Bag packed (notebook, pen, ID, any required documents)
- Chargers ready
- Alarm set — and a backup alarm

Remove friction from tomorrow morning. Decision fatigue is real. Every decision you don't have to make at 7am is energy available for Day 1.

---

### 9pm — Wind Down (rest of the evening)

No screens after 9pm if you can manage it. Or at minimum, nothing work-related.

Read a book. Watch something. Take a walk. The point is to move your nervous system away from anticipation mode and toward rest.

**The two things that actually help performance tomorrow:**
1. Sleep (7-9 hours if possible)
2. Moderate exercise today (already done or a short evening walk)

**The things that don't help:**
- Reading more about the company at 11pm
- Reviewing notes you've already internalized
- Trying to solve any technical problem tonight

---

### Sleep Protocol

- Aim for lights out by 10-10:30pm
- If you're anxious and can't sleep: that's normal. Don't fight it. Breathe slowly. The rest itself has value even without sleep.
- Avoid alcohol — it disrupts sleep architecture even if it helps you fall asleep faster

---

## A Final Note

You started this plan on Day 1 as someone preparing for a search. You're ending it as someone who built real, tested competence across 13 weeks.

The 89 days weren't just interview prep. They were a systematic investment in how you think about distributed systems, financial data, algorithmic problem-solving, and professional communication.

That investment doesn't expire when the interview ends. It's yours.

Show up tomorrow as someone who knows what they know, is honest about what they don't, and is genuinely interested in building something.

That's exactly the kind of person they hired.
