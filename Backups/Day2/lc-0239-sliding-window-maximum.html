Visual Guide: Sliding Window Maximum

Array: [1, 3, -1, -3, 5] | K: 3

The "Aha!" Moment: Use a Monotonic Deque. When a new big number arrives, any smaller earlier numbers are instantly useless because the big number will outlast them all. Kick them out of the back of the queue!

Step 1: Process '1', then '3'

'1' enters. But when '3' arrives, '3' is bigger! '1' is kicked out of the back.

Deque:
3 MAXIdx: 1
← '1' was evicted!

Step 2: Process '-1' (Window Size reached K=3)

[1, 3, -1] -3, 5

'-1' arrives. It's smaller than '3', so it gets in line safely.

Deque:
3 MAXIdx: 1
-1 Idx: 2
Return: 3

Step 3: Process '-3'

1, [3, -1, -3], 5

'-3' is smaller than '-1', so it gets in line safely at the back.

Deque:
3 MAXIdx: 1
-1 Idx: 2
-3 Idx: 3
Return: 3

Step 4: Process '5' (The Muscle Arrives)

1, 3, [-1, -3, 5]

First: Idx 1 ('3') expires out of the left window! Pop it from the front.

Then: '5' arrives. It is bigger than '-3' AND '-1'. It violently evicts them both from the back.

Deque:
5 MAXIdx: 4
← -3 & -1 evicted!
Return: 5
