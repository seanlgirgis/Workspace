Visual Guide: Min Stack

The "Aha!" Moment: You can't just store one `min_val` variable, because what happens when you pop that minimum value away? How do you know what the minimum was before it?

The answer: Every item pushed gets a "Buddy" pushed to a parallel stack. That buddy records the absolute minimum value at that exact moment in time.

Main Stack

-3
0
-2

push(-2)
push(0)
push(-3)

Min (Buddy) Stack

-3
-2
-2

min(-2, none) = -2
min(0, -2) = -2
min(-3, -2) = -3

State Execution

Right now, getMin() will look at the top of the Min Stack and return -3.

If we execute POP(), the top element of BOTH stacks are destroyed.

The new top of the main stack will be 0. But look at its buddy! The new top of the Min Stack immediately falls back to -2. The historical minimum is perfectly preserved!
